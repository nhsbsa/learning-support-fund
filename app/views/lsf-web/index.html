Iteration 5

Travel and Dual Accommodation Expenses (TDAE) - Version 3 Implementing the journey for BSA staff to process TDAE claims after HEIs have completed their checks. Updating BSA Admin toolkit and improving user journeys.

What we created:

  • Manage users journey improved with more accessible components and seaparating questions across pages.
  • A read-only version of a student claim including all TDAE expenses.
  • A student claim that includes all TDAE expenses - This is Emmit Mar's claim.
  • A student claim that includes both Taxi and Overseas costs (including Insurance and Visa Fees) - This is Alyssa Geary's claim.
  • A student claim that includes Accommodation and Accommodation Travel expenses - This is Daniel Jackson's claim.
  • The ability for CCS Agents to add a feedback note to the student's claim, as well as a general note to the student's record.
  • The screens which include the claim dates and claim value - These appear on the student's course details under the TDAE tab, as well as on the Task List within individual claims.

What we plan to test:

  • The read-only version of a student claim.
  • The student claim that includes both Taxi and Overseas costs (including Insurance and Visa Fees) - This is Alyssa Geary's claim.
  • A student claim that includes Accommodation and Accommodation Travel expenses - This is Daniel Jackson's claim.
  • The ability for CCS Agents to add a feedback note to the student's claim, as well as a general note to the student's record.
  • The screens which include the claim dates and claim value - These appear on the student's course details under the TDAE tab, as well as on the Task List within individual claims.

What we found:

  • NHSBSA Processors and CCS Agents like that they're able to view a read-only version of a claim as will help answer student's queries e.g., if they've spotted a duplicate claim/ticket warning and want to double check this.
  • NHSBSA Processors appreciate the types of information we collect from the students r.e., their taxi journey/costs. By providing details around who it was authorised by, and the reason why a taxi was needed is very helpful information and will help NHSBSA to process taxi claims more easily with the context/detail provided.
  • CCS Agents find it easier to identify the right claim by checking both the claim date and claim value with the student.
  • CCS Agents and NHSBSA Processors like how easy it is to add a feedback/general note to the student's claim or record. They like the transparency this will create.

Any known issues?

  • Not all examples of uploaded evidence are representative of the cost they're linked to. There is a parking ticket used as a generic example of evidence simply to show the functionality.
