Iteration 8

Travel and Dual Accommodation Expenses (TDAE) - Version 4

What we created:

  • TDAE Version 4 Taxi journey
  • TDAE Version 4 Overseas costs journey
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
  • Bank details change request
  • Moved payment details entry to after funding approval (WIP)
  • Add placement travel dates to placement travel details after costs have been added. Single address journey and multiple addresses journey.

What we plan to test:

  • TDAE Version 4 Taxi journey
  • TDAE Version 4 Overseas costs journey

What we found:

  • The vast majority of students previously knew that the likes of Taxis and Overseas costs need some form of authorisation. However, students like how clear this information is communicated throughout TDAE, particularly on the 'What costs did you pay for?' screen within the details drop-down.
  • Students appreciate the ability to provide context around their taxi journey/cost. For example, the reason why they needed a taxi.
  • Students find it much easier, and more straightforward, to claim back Overseas costs using the digital platform. They find the content provided throughout the user journey helpful, and would find it easier than using the paper PDF form to claim these expenses back.

Any known issues?

  • Some users have failed to initially notice the 'Add international address' link at the top of the Enter placement address screen. Once they've entered in what details they could into the fields available, it is then, when they've scrolled to the bottom of the screen that there's no 'Country' input field. We deliberately placed the link at the top of the screen, above the input fields, for two reasons:

    1) For accessibility - to avoid screen-readers reading out all input fields and the user jumping the gun, and going ahead an entering details before the screen-reader has finished/reached the bottom of the screen where the 'Add an international address' link at the very bottom of the screen As this would cause the user frustration after they've entered all details to then find out there was an option to specifically enter an international address.
    2) To avoid users inputting/squeezing their international address into the regular UK address fields and then realising that there was an option to enter an international address via a button located at the bottom.

    The UCD team are aware of this issue and will keep an eye on feedback regarding this particular issue. Future recommendations may include making the link more obvious e.g., a secondary button.
  • Not all evidence is representative of the associated cost - more to show functionality.
